Introduction

Every long game
needs a game plan.

Something has changed for the better in Silicon Valley. As the balance of power has shifted in favor of founders, technology companies are staying private longer, and founders are keeping control of their companies.

As such, these founders are beginning to wrestle with the hard question of how to go about building a company that doesn't merely scale, but keeps on innovating for a very long time, in other words, a company that endures.

Until now, there’s been very little said publicly on this question. Most startup advice is on how to start small and scale up, but what's it like to build a technology company that can last for the long run and never stop innovating?

This series is the first public exchange we know of that hopes to provide contemporary answers to this valuable question.

Please join us.
